The Arts Council (An Chomhairle Ealaíon) is the statutory body established under the Arts Act 1951 and reconstituted under the Arts Act 2003, responsible for developing and promoting the arts in Ireland. Under the Department of Tourism, Culture, Arts, Gaeltacht, Sport and Media, it distributes exchequer funding to individual artists, arts organisations, and local authorities across disciplines including theatre, music, visual arts, literature, dance, film, and circus. The Arts Council is also the principal adviser to government on arts policy. In 2024 it employed approximately 122 staff (whole-time equivalent) and recorded total income of approximately €134 million, almost entirely from Oireachtas grants, of which the large majority was disbursed as grants to the arts sector.
